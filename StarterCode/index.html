In Poverty (%) vs Obese(%)

There is a slight upstream trend one can see form the graph. There could be a correlation between the Poverty (%) and the Obese(%). Factors that may affect the trend might be that having a healthy diet or access to a health instructor is more expensive and less accessible for people with lower income and probably want to save up that money for more vital stuff they might need.

In Poverty (%) vs Smokes(%)

One can see an upstream trend correlation between the Poverty (%) and the smokers. So, one could say that the higher the Poverty (%), the most likely is that state to have a higher smoking rate. Some factors that may come to may that could affect is that in poverty has an obvious effect on a person's happiness, more so in a country like the US, so seeking compensators through other ways may cause more people to find smoking as a way to help them get through.

This one was probably the most painful one. This one is the scatter plot with the highest upstream slope out of the 3 poverty realted plots, as we know; The US health system is very not perfect, and one of the defects is that is very expensive. This hits the people with lower incomes the hardest. So, one could say that the higher the poverty(%), the less likely is that one has Healthcare.
